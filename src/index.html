Riga the capital city of Latvia and largest city in Baltic states, is situated on the Baltic Sea coast on the mouth of the river Daugava. Climate neutrality has become one of the most important priority. Riga has set strong foundation towards climate neutrality and has reached CO2 emission reduction until 2020 by 61,8%.

However Riga has become one of the 100 cities that will become climate-neutral smart cities by 2030 which sets new challenges. Riga is ready to keep moving forward, are you ready to get involved ?

Workshop on innovative procurement

On November 10, the second stakeholder workshop “Yesterday’s solutions to today’s problems. Creating a road map for implementing innovative procurement in practice” was held in Riga. The event’s focus was to bring together representatives from the private and public sectors to highlight the main challenges and seek potential solutions for implementing innovative procurement in Latvia.

Workshop on UM governance

On September 14, the first stakeholder workshop on urban mobility governance towards climate neutrality took place. The focus of this workshop was to identify and address the Latvian Mission cities – Rīga and Liepāja urban mobility challenges in a Mission lead approach and emphasize the multi-level stakeholder cooperation in the cities path toward climate neutrality.
